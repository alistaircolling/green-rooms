Green Rooms is the UK’s first arts hotel, a social enterprise that offers affordable accommodation aimed at people working in the arts.
We are excellently located opposite Wood Green tube station, just 12 minutes from King’s Cross and 20 minutes into Central London direct by tube.
We focus on what you really do need. great beds, decent showers, and friendly and knowledgeable creative staff (who all live locally and can tell you exactly where’s good to go). We have easy access to wi-fi and we serve proper coffee and fresh pastries to get you going in the morning. There’s nothing too fancy, and no TV sets in the rooms. We’re ideal for people who want to make the most of London, and just want a simple and chic place to rest during their stay. Why pay for things you don’t need? Save your pocket money for those irresistible gallery gift shops. We’re more special than a hostel, and more hearty than a chain hotel.
Green Rooms has a lobby bar with local beers, homemade cakes and cocktails. The interior was designed by our co-founder Kurt Bredenbeck and theatre producer Mandeep Tutt. Architects Soda created the interior (we're located in a re-purposed art deco office block) and fashion label Folk designed some of the furniture in the rooms.
We also have a gallery and a bustling diary of arts events. Join our newsletter and be the first to hear about what’s going on here. We hope to meet you soon!
We’re home to Colombian Street Kitchen on the ground floor. Visitors can host their London friends here and chat over a tasty plate of empanadas. We hope our guests will interact and engage with one another, have conversations and maybe even collaborate.
